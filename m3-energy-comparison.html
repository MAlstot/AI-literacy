M3.3 — The Hidden Cost of a Prompt: AI Energy Comparison

TRAIL • Module 3: AI and Ethics • Baylor University

Key framing: The environmental story of AI is neither simple reassurance nor simple alarm. Per-query AI consumption is modest; at the scale of billions of daily queries and the training of frontier models, it becomes significant. Toggle between views to see the honest picture.
Energy per Single Use/Event (Wh)
Comparing individual AI queries to everyday energy consumption activities
💡 What this shows A single ChatGPT query (~0.001–0.01 kWh) consumes more energy than a Google search but less than leaving a ceiling fan on for 20 minutes. Per-use, AI is not catastrophic — but scale changes the picture dramatically.
Large-Scale & Lifecycle Energy Comparison (MWh or equivalent)
Training runs, annual AI data center demand, and lifecycle comparisons
💡 What this shows Training a large language model can emit as much CO 2 as five transatlantic flights. Total AI electricity demand is projected to double by 2026 (IEA, 2024). The Jevons Paradox warning: efficiency gains may simply enable more use at greater aggregate scale.
⚠️ Data note: Energy figures for AI vary significantly across sources, model sizes, hardware, and data center efficiency. Values shown are best available estimates from cited sources; ranges reflect genuine uncertainty. Some comparisons (e.g., EV lifecycle vs. AI training) involve contested methodological assumptions. Treat as illustrative of order of magnitude, not exact equivalents.
