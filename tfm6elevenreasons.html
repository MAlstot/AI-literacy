The Eleven Reasons: A Mentoring Matrix

Teach Forward · Module 6 · Adapted from Bertram Gallant & Rettinger (2025) and Megan Alstot’s Teaching for Integrity materials

Eleven reasons. One story. Cheating has always existed; AI did not change why, it changed how.

Research names eleven reasons students cheat. Each comes with a question worth asking of your assignment, the conditions that feed it, and the integrity builders that answer it. Filter by pairing or open any reason to see the full matrix. When you’re ready, the self-diagnostic on this page turns it on your own assignment.

No blame here. Most good assignments invite two to four of these without anyone intending it. Noticing is the mentoring move, and good teaching is still the best integrity builder.

Pairings (Communication · Consistent Values · Flexibility & Accountability · Design & Culture) group the reasons by the instructional response they share.
