Step 1 · Review

The Five Levels at a Glance

Tap each level to expand. Notice how the 4Ds intensify as you move right: what you delegate expands, the discernment students must exercise deepens, and the diligence (documentation) expected grows.

1 Level 1 — No AI Use
Students complete the work entirely without AI. Best for: assessing foundational knowledge and skills students must own independently. 4Ds connection: the delegation decision is "delegate nothing" — and that's a deliberate, defensible choice, not a default.
2 Level 2 — Limited AI Use
AI is permitted for narrow, specified tasks (brainstorming, feedback on drafts) while core work remains the student's. 4Ds connection: description skills begin to matter; students acknowledge where AI assisted.
3 Level 3 — Collaborative AI Use
Students work with AI as a thought partner throughout the task — generating, critiquing, and revising — while adapting or rejecting its contributions with justification. 4Ds connection: discernment is the heart of this level; documentation of the collaboration becomes expected.
4 Level 4 — Integrated AI Use
AI is woven into the workflow the way it is in professional practice — purposeful tool selection, strategic prompting, and critical synthesis of outputs. 4Ds connection: all four Ds operate together; diligence expands to transcripts and rationale for AI decisions.
5 Level 5 — AI Innovation
Students use AI to create novel approaches, products, or insights — exploring what the technology makes newly possible in the discipline. 4Ds connection: delegation and description at their most sophisticated; diligence includes ethical analysis of the innovation itself.
