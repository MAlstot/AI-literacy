Start here

You didn't come to graduate school to have a chatbot do your thinking. You came to build something — a way of thinking, a set of capabilities, a credential that means something because you earned it. AI can either accelerate that build or quietly hollow it out. The difference is fluency.

One thing before we start: fluency doesn't presume enthusiasm. If you've barely touched AI, or you have real reservations about using it at all, you are not behind — and this activity never asks you to use AI. Deciding that a task (or a season of your education) should stay AI-free is itself a fluency decision, and your reasons belong in this conversation as much as anyone's.

When we meet, we'll dig into two questions together:

Ethical engagementWhat does honest, responsible AI use actually mean for a graduate student?
Imagination for useWhat could AI make possible in your learning — without short-circuiting the thinking you came here to develop?

Must 1 · Watch

The AI Fluency 4D Framework (~30 min of short videos)

Our session is built on the AI Fluency Framework — four competencies (the "4Ds") developed by Rick Dakan, Joseph Feller, and Anthropic. Watch these six short lessons (~5 minutes each) from Anthropic's free video course. Take 1 and 2 first; then the four Ds in any order, holding the question that goes with each:

  1. Lesson 1: Introduction to AI Fluency · ~5 minWhat does "fluency" mean beyond knowing how to prompt?
  2. Lesson 2B: The 4D Framework · ~5 minThe map for our whole session — all four Ds in one picture.
  3. Lesson 4: A closer look at Delegation · ~5 minWhat thinking did I come to grad school to build — and what can I safely share with AI?
  4. Lesson 6: A closer look at Description · ~5 minCan I tell AI what I actually want — context, constraints, and its role?
  5. Lesson 8: A closer look at Discernment · 5:23How will I judge what comes back — against what I know, not just how it sounds?
  6. Lesson 10: A closer look at Diligence · ~5 minAm I willing to own, verify, and disclose everything I use?

Want to keep going? The full 12-video course playlist adds lessons on how generative AI works and effective prompting.
